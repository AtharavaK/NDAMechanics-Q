NDA Mechanics Questions and Answers

1. Scalars and Vector Quantities

Q1: Which of the following is a vector quantity?

(a) Speed
(b) Distance
(c) Displacement
(d) Mass
Answer: (c) Displacement
Explanation: Displacement has both magnitude and direction, making it a vector quantity.

Q2: Two forces of \(6 \, \text{N}\) and \(8 \, \text{N}\) act at a point at an angle of \(90^\circ\). What is the magnitude of the resultant force?

(a) \(10 \, \text{N}\)
(b) \(14 \, \text{N}\)
(c) \(2 \, \text{N}\)
(d) \(12 \, \text{N}\)
Answer: (a) \(10 \, \text{N}\)
Solution: Using the Pythagorean theorem: \[ R = \sqrt{F_1^2 + F_2^2} = \sqrt{6^2 + 8^2} = \sqrt{36 + 64} = 10 \, \text{N}. \]

2. Kinematics

Q3: Which of the following remains constant during uniform circular motion?

(a) Velocity
(b) Speed
(c) Acceleration
(d) Displacement
Answer: (b) Speed
Explanation: In uniform circular motion, speed remains constant, but velocity changes due to the change in direction.

Q4: A car accelerates uniformly from rest to \(20 \, \text{m/s}\) in \(5 \, \text{s}\). What is the acceleration?

(a) \(2 \, \text{m/s}^2\)
(b) \(4 \, \text{m/s}^2\)
(c) \(5 \, \text{m/s}^2\)
(d) \(10 \, \text{m/s}^2\)
Answer: (b) \(4 \, \text{m/s}^2\)
Solution: Using the formula for acceleration: \[ a = \frac{\Delta v}{\Delta t} = \frac{20 - 0}{5} = 4 \, \text{m/s}^2. \]

3. Laws of Motion

Q5: According to Newton’s Third Law, action and reaction forces:

(a) Act on the same body
(b) Act on different bodies
(c) Cancel each other
(d) Are always equal to zero
Answer: (b) Act on different bodies
Explanation: Action and reaction forces act on different bodies and never cancel each other.

Q6: A force of \(15 \, \text{N}\) acts on a body of mass \(3 \, \text{kg}\). What is the acceleration produced?

(a) \(3 \, \text{m/s}^2\)
(b) \(5 \, \text{m/s}^2\)
(c) \(10 \, \text{m/s}^2\)
(d) \(15 \, \text{m/s}^2\)
Answer: (b) \(5 \, \text{m/s}^2\)
Solution: Using Newton's Second Law: \[ a = \frac{F}{m} = \frac{15}{3} = 5 \, \text{m/s}^2. \]

4. Work, Energy, and Power

Q7: A force of \(10 \, \text{N}\) displaces an object by \(5 \, \text{m}\) in the direction of the force. What is the work done?

(a) \(20 \, \text{J}\)
(b) \(50 \, \text{J}\)
(c) \(100 \, \text{J}\)
(d) \(150 \, \text{J}\)
Answer: (b) \(50 \, \text{J}\)
Solution: Using the formula for work: \[ W = Fd = (10)(5) = 50 \, \text{J}. \]

Q8: A machine lifts a load of \(200 \, \text{kg}\) to a height of \(10 \, \text{m}\) in \(5 \, \text{s}\). What is the power of the machine? (\(g = 10 \, \text{m/s}^2\))

(a) \(2000 \, \text{W}\)
(b) \(3000 \, \text{W}\)
(c) \(4000 \, \text{W}\)
(d) \(5000 \, \text{W}\)
Answer: (c) \(4000 \,
Answer: (c) \(4000 \, \text{W}\)
Solution: Using the formula for power: \[ P = \frac{W}{t} = \frac{mgh}{t} = \frac{(200)(10)(10)}{5} = 4000 \, \text{W}. \]

5. Rotational Motion

Q9: A stone tied to a string of length \(1 \, \text{m}\) is whirled in a horizontal circle with a speed of \(4 \, \text{m/s}\). What is the centripetal force if the mass of the stone is \(0.5 \, \text{kg}\)?

(a) \(4 \, \text{N}\)
(b) \(6 \, \text{N}\)
(c) \(8 \, \text{N}\)
(d) \(10 \, \text{N}\)
Answer: (c) \(8 \, \text{N}\)
Solution: Using the formula for centripetal force: \[ F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r} = \frac{(0.5)(4)^2}{1} = 8 \, \text{N}. \]

Q10: A torque of \(10 \, \text{Nm}\) is applied to a wheel of moment of inertia \(2 \, \text{kg} \cdot \text{m}^2\). What is the angular acceleration?

(a) \(2 \, \text{rad/s}^2\)
(b) \(5 \, \text{rad/s}^2\)
(c) \(10 \, \text{rad/s}^2\)
(d) \(20 \, \text{rad/s}^2\)
Answer: (b) \(5 \, \text{rad/s}^2\)
Solution: Using the formula for angular acceleration: \[ \alpha = \frac{\tau}{I} = \frac{10}{2} = 5 \, \text{rad/s}^2. \]

6. Gravitation

Q11: Two masses of \(5 \, \text{kg}\) and \(10 \, \text{kg}\) are separated by \(2 \, \text{m}\). What is the gravitational force between them? (\(G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \, \text{Nm}^2/\text{kg}^2\))

(a) \(8.34 \times 10^{-10} \, \text{N}\)
(b) \(1.67 \times 10^{-9} \, \text{N}\)
(c) \(2.50 \times 10^{-10} \, \text{N}\)
(d) \(4.17 \times 10^{-10} \, \text{N}\)
Answer: (a) \(8.34 \times 10^{-10} \, \text{N}\)
Solution: Using Newton's Law of Gravitation: \[ F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} = (6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \frac{(5)(10)}{(2)^2} = 8.34 \times 10^{-10} \, \text{N}. \]

Q12: What is the escape velocity from Earth’s surface? (\(M = 6 \times 10^{24} \, \text{kg}\), \(R = 6.4 \times 10^6 \, \text{m}\), \(G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \, \text{Nm}^2/\text{kg}^2\))

(a) \(10.2 \, \text{km/s}\)
(b) \(11.2 \, \text{km/s}\)
(c) \(12.2 \, \text{km/s}\)
(d) \(13.2 \, \text{km/s}\)
Answer: (b) \(11.2 \, \text{km/s}\)
Solution: Using the formula for escape velocity: \[ v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{R}} = \sqrt{\frac{2(6.67 \times 10^{-11})(6 \times 10^{24})}{6.4 \times 10^6}} \approx 11.2 \, \text{km/s}. \]

8. Oscillations and Waves

Q15: What type of wave is sound?

(a) Transverse
(b) Longitudinal
(c) Electromagnetic
(d) Surface
Answer: (b) Longitudinal
Explanation: Sound waves are longitudinal waves.

Q16: A pendulum has a length of \(1 \, \text{m}\). What is its time period? (\(g = 9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2\))

(a) \(1 \, \text{s}\)
(b) \(2 \, \text{s}\)
(c) \(3 \, \text{s}\)
(d) \(4 \, \text{s}\)
Answer: (b) \(2 \, \text{s}\)
Solution: Using the formula for the time period of a pendulum: \[ T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{L}{g}} = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{1}{9.8}} \approx 2 \, \text{s}. \]

9. Center of Mass

Q17: Where is the center of mass of a uniform rod located?

(a) At one end
(b) At the midpoint
(c) Outside the rod
(d) At the quarter point
Answer: (b) At the midpoint
Explanation: For a uniform rod, the center of mass lies at its geometric center (midpoint).

Q18: Does the center of mass always lie within the body?

(a) Yes
(b) No
(c) Only for solids
(d) Only for fluids
Answer: (b) No
Explanation: No, e.g., a ring’s center of mass lies at its center, outside the material.

10. Collisions

Q19: In which type of collision is kinetic energy conserved?

(a) Elastic
(b) Inelastic
(c) Perfectly inelastic
(d) None of these
Answer: (a) Elastic
Explanation: Kinetic energy is conserved only in elastic collisions.

Q20: A \(2 \, \text{kg}\) ball moving at \(3 \, \text{m/s}\) collides with a stationary \(1 \, \text{kg}\) ball. After the collision, the \(2 \, \text{kg}\) ball moves at \(1 \, \text{m/s}\). What is the velocity of the \(1 \, \text{kg}\) ball?

(a) \(2 \, \text{m/s}\)
(b) \(3 \, \text{m/s}\)
(c) \(4 \, \text{m/s}\)
(d) \(5 \, \text{m/s}\)
Answer: (c) \(4 \, \text{m/s}\)
Solution: Using conservation of momentum: \[ m_1u_1 + m_2u_2 = m_1v_1 + m_2v_2 \implies (2)(3) + (1)(0) = (2)(1) + (1)v_2. \] \[ 6 = 2 + v_2 \implies v_2 = 4 \, \text{m/s}. \]

Q21: What happens to the gravitational force between two objects if the distance between them is halved?

(a) Doubles
(b) Halves
(c) Becomes four times
(d) Becomes one-fourth
Answer: (c) Becomes four times
Explanation: \(F \propto \frac{1}{r^2}\). If \(r\) is halved, \(F\) becomes four times.

Q22: Why do astronauts feel weightless in space?

(a) No gravity exists in space
(b) They are in free fall around Earth
(c) Their mass becomes zero
(d) Air resistance cancels gravity
Answer: (b) They are in free fall around Earth
Explanation: Astronauts feel weightless because they are in continuous free fall around Earth.

Q36: A solid cube of side \(0.1 \, \text{m}\) is completely submerged in water. What is the buoyant force acting on it? (\(\rho_{\text{water}} = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\), \(g = 10 \, \text{m/s}^2\))

(b) \(10 \, \text{N}\)
(c) \(100 \, \text{N}\)
(d) \(1000 \, \text{N}\)
Answer: (b) \(10 \, \text{N}\)

Q37: The time period of a simple pendulum depends on:

Q41: A gas expands and does \(200 \, \text{J}\) of work on the surroundings while absorbing \(300 \, \text{J}\) of heat. What is the change in internal energy?

(a) \(100 \, \text{J}\)
(b) \(200 \, \text{J}\)
(c) \(300 \, \text{J}\)
(d) \(500 \, \text{J}\)
Answer: (a) \(100 \, \text{J}\)
Solution: Using the First Law of Thermodynamics: \[ \Delta U = Q - W = 300 - 200 = 100 \, \text{J}. \]

Answer: (c) Bernoulli’s Principle
Explanation: Bernoulli’s Principle explains how faster-moving air above the wing creates lower pressure, generating lift.

Q43: A liquid flows through a pipe of varying cross-sectional area. If the velocity at a point where the cross-sectional area is \(2 \, \text{cm}^2\) is \(4 \, \text{m/s}\), what is the velocity at a point where the cross-sectional area is \(1 \, \text{cm}^2\)?

(a) \(2 \, \text{m/s}\)
(b) \(4 \, \text{m/s}\)
(c) \(8 \, \text{m/s}\)
(d) \(16 \, \text{m/s}\)
Answer: (c) \(8 \, \text{m/s}\)
Solution: Using the equation of continuity \(A_1v_1 = A_2v_2\): \[ (2)(4) = (1)v_2 \implies v_2 = 8 \, \text{m/s}. \]

Q45: A disc of radius \(0.5 \, \text{m}\) and mass \(2 \, \text{kg}\) rotates with an angular velocity of \(10 \, \text{rad/s}\). What is its rotational kinetic energy?

(a) \(10 \, \text{J}\)
(b) \(25 \, \text{J}\)
(c) \(50 \, \text{J}\)
(d) \(100 \, \text{J}\)
Answer: (b) \(25 \, \text{J}\)
Solution: Rotational kinetic energy \(KE = \frac{1}{2}I\omega^2\), where \(I = \frac{1}{2}MR^2\): \[ KE = \frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{1}{2}(2)(0.5)^2\right)(10)^2 = 25 \, \text{J}. \]

Q50: A satellite orbits Earth at a height equal to the Earth’s radius. What is the acceleration due to gravity at that height? (\(g_{\text{surface}} = 9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2\))

(a) \(2.45 \, \text{m/s}^2\)
(b) \(4.9 \, \text{m/s}^2\)
(c) \(9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2\)
(d) \(19.6 \, \text{m/s}^2\)
Answer: (a) \(2.45 \, \text{m/s}^2\)
Solution: Acceleration due to gravity at height \(h\) is given by: \[ g_h = \frac{g_{\text{surface}}}{\left(1 + \frac{h}{R}\right)^2}. \] Here, \(h = R\), so: \[ g_h = \frac{9.8}{(1 + 1)^2} = \frac{9.8}{4} = 2.45 \, \text{m/s}^2. \]
